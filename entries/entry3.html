Alter Egos

If I had to sum myself up in one word, it would be obsession. Whenever I commit to something, I go all-in. Whether it’s delivering a project, overhauling my wardrobe, or diving into a new sport — I immerse myself completely. I’ve always seen that as a strength. Just look at top athletes: they’re not casual about greatness. They’re consistent, focused, ruthless in their pursuit. The idea of entering ‘the zone’ — of becoming a different human being like Kobe’s Black Mamba persona — hits something deep in me.

I believe you can define an alter ego based on the results you want to achieve — and live it. It’s like flipping a switch. You craft the identity, build rituals to step into it, and train yourself to stay there. The gym is a perfect example: you take your pre-workout, put on your music, and go to war with the weights. You stop being your normal self — you become the version that gets it done.

Professionally, it’s no different. You can embody a certain character: through how you dress, how you speak, how you attack your tasks. It sharpens you. It forces you to understand the rules of the game, pick your strategy, execute, and then reflect and optimize. That kind of clarity — of becoming the person who achieves the goal — is where alter egos show their true power.

But here’s the catch: that mindset doesn’t always play nice with real life. The very traits that make you a machine — tunnel vision, emotional detachment, intensity — can make you hard to be around. When you’re deep in the zone, connection feels like a distraction. You cut people off. You withdraw. You armor up to fight through resistance, but that same armor blocks intimacy, fun, and lightness.

And so the real question is: how do you hold onto the power of the alter ego — the sharpness, the focus — without losing your humanity? Is there a way to shift gears, to step in and out of that zone with intention? What if mastery isn’t just about being obsessive — but learning when to drop it? What if the real high-performance move is knowing how to integrate, not isolate?
